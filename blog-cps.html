How to Boost Your CPS (Clicks Per Second)

Published on February 2025 by Hitbar Team

Clicks Per Second (CPS) is a measure of how many times you can press a key, usually the spacebar or mouse button, in one second. Many gamers compete to achieve high CPS scores, and it’s a fun way to test speed and accuracy.

1. Use Proper Finger Technique

The fastest players usually use two fingers alternating rapidly on the spacebar. This reduces strain and increases speed compared to using only one finger.

2. Try Butterfly and Jitter Clicking

Butterfly clicking involves tapping with two fingers alternately, while jitter clicking uses quick muscle vibrations. Both methods can improve CPS significantly, but they require practice.

3. Get a Responsive Keyboard

Not all keyboards are created equal. Mechanical keyboards, especially with red or blue switches, are much faster and more responsive for CPS tests than regular membrane keyboards.

4. Practice Daily

Consistency is the key. Even just 5 minutes of daily practice on Hitbar can help you see major improvements in your score within a week.

5. Track Your Progress

Keep testing yourself on Hitbar and record your scores. Over time, you’ll notice your CPS improving if you stick with the practice.

Conclusion

Improving CPS is a mix of technique, equipment, and regular practice. Use the methods above, and challenge your friends to see who can become the fastest player!
